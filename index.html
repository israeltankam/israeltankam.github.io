Research summary

Statement: My research interests lie at the intersection of mathematical modelling, plant disease dynamics, and sustainable agriculture. I am particularly focused on developing advanced mathematical frameworks to understand and manage the dynamics of viral, fungal, and pest-driven epidemics, with a recent emphasis on population demo-genetics for resistance durability. My work aims to develop, analyse and translate complex mathematical models into practical decision-making tools that empower agricultural stakeholders, enhancing crop resilience and optimizing management strategies. I have experience with various computational methods, including simulation and optimization techniques, which I apply to tackle challenges in pest and plant disease management, particularly for species such as the potato cyst nematode and banana soil-borne pests and cassava viruses. Through interdisciplinary collaboration and innovative modelling approaches, I aspire to contribute to the field by addressing real-world agricultural challenges while advancing our theoretical understanding of disease dynamics.

Fields: Mathematical Modelling, Ecological Modelling, Plant disease epidemiology, Dynamical Systems, Biomathematics, Computational Modelling and Simulations, Optimal Control, Optimization, Numerical Analysis, Decision Support System, Computational Biology.
